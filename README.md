# 🏥 Diabetes Risk Analysis Dashboard

## 📌 Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze patient healthcare data to identify key factors influencing diabetes risk and provide actionable insights for early detection and prevention.

## ❗ Problem Statement
Diabetes is a growing health concern worldwide. Identifying high-risk patients based on medical conditions and lifestyle factors is crucial for early intervention. This project aims to analyze patterns in patient data to understand the impact of factors like BMI, HbA1c level, smoking, age, and hypertension on diabetes.

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- SQL (MySQL)
- Power BI
- Data Cleaning & Analysis

## 📊 Dashboard Features
- Total Patients Overview
- Diabetic vs Non-Diabetic Analysis
- Smoking Impact on Diabetes
- Risk Level Segmentation (High Risk, Pre-Diabetic, Low Risk)
- Age Group Analysis
- Health Condition Impact (Hypertension)
- Average BMI & HbA1c Comparison

## 🔍 Key Findings & Insights

- Diabetic patients have significantly higher average BMI compared to non-diabetic patients.
- HbA1c level is a strong indicator of diabetes risk.
- Patients with hypertension show a higher likelihood of being diabetic.
- Senior age group has the highest number of diabetic patients.
- Smoking history also influences diabetes risk, especially among former and current smokers.

## 💡 Recommendations

- Promote healthy lifestyle and weight management to reduce BMI levels.
- Regular screening of HbA1c and blood glucose levels for early detection.
- Special attention should be given to patients with hypertension.
- Awareness campaigns targeting older age groups.
- Encourage smoking cessation programs.

## 🧾 Conclusion

This project demonstrates how healthcare data can be used to identify high-risk individuals and support better decision-making. The insights derived can help healthcare professionals take preventive actions and improve patient outcomes.
